Research Agenda

Core questions

The long-term questions guiding my work

Actionable measurement

How can we measure learning in ways that are valid, interpretable, and feasible for everyday classrooms?

Evidence standards for EdTech & AI classrooms

What should count as evidence for educational technologies—especially AI-mediated learning experiences?

Scale without drift

How do we translate evidence into scalable systems without losing fidelity under real constraints?

Methods & lenses

A toolkit for reproducible evidence

Measurement & calibration

IRT/BKT, validity/reliability checks, rater consistency, minimal detectable change, and fairness audits (e.g., DIF).

Experimental & causal

A/B testing, variance reduction (CUPED), heterogeneity analysis, ANCOVA/baseline adjustment, matching & DiD.

Process data & monitoring

Event logging and data contracts, time-window modeling, confidence calibration, subgroup monitoring, and error taxonomies.

Implementation translation

Turning evidence into operational artifacts: SOPs, dashboards, rubrics, and briefs + technical annexes.

Near-term directions

What I’m building next

From outcome to process: expand beyond test and survey outcomes into richer process traces (learning paths, interaction patterns, teacher–AI–student dynamics).

Compensatory effects at scale: identify when AI-enabled systems reduce gaps (vs. reinforcing them), and validate mechanisms in realistic settings.

Evaluation infrastructure: build reusable “evidence gates” for product iteration—metrics, guardrails, and protocols that survive scaling.
